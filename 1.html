HISTORY

Anna University, established on September 4, 1978, by the Government of Tamil Nadu, emerged through the integration of four prestigious institutions in Chennai: the College of Engineering, Guindy (CEG), the Alagappa College of Technology (ACT), the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), and the School of Architecture and Planning (SAP). Named after C.N. Annadurai, a revered former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, the university initially operated as a unitary type of institution. Over time, it expanded its programs and began affiliating engineering colleges across Tamil Nadu, significantly increasing its influence. In 2001, it transitioned into an affiliating university, further broadening its reach. The university experienced a split in 2007 into six universities, only to be reunited in 2010, reaffirming its status as a single, cohesive entity. Known for its robust academic programs, research initiatives, and industry collaborations, Anna University celebrated its silver jubilee in 2003. It continues to uphold its legacy of excellence in engineering and technology education, contributing significantly to the development of skilled professionals in India.

INFRASTRUCTURE

Anna University boasts a comprehensive and modern infrastructure designed to support its extensive academic and research activities. The university's main campus is located in Guindy, Chennai, with additional campuses including the College of Engineering, Guindy (CEG), the Alagappa College of Technology (ACT), the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in Chromepet, and the School of Architecture and Planning (SAP). The campuses feature numerous academic buildings, well-equipped classrooms, lecture halls, seminar rooms, and specialized labs. State-of-the-art libraries, such as the Central Library at the Guindy campus, provide vast collections of books, journals, and e-resources. The university hosts dedicated research centers focusing on fields like biotechnology, nanotechnology, and automotive research, equipped with advanced facilities. Modern laboratories support practical learning in various engineering disciplines. Residential facilities include separate hostels for men and women, offering amenities like dining, recreation areas, and internet connectivity. Additionally, the university encourages physical fitness with extensive sports facilities.
